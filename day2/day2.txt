When you click a link , Angular calls history.pushState({}, '', '/products'). This adds /products to the browser's history stack without reloading .
When the user presses the "Back" button , the browser fires a popstate event.
Angular's Router listens to the popstate event.
The Router reads the current URL (/products) from the adress bar 
the Router matches /products against your routes array
The Router destroys the contact Component and instantiates the products Component inside <router-outlet>
